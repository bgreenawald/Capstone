HE Dr Mohammed Matar al Kaabi, Awqaf's Chairman, stressed that the naming of HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice President and Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai, "the Cultural Personality of the Year" by the Sheikh Zayed Book Award is a deserved honouring to the UAE and to an exceptional figure who made prominent achievements through his insightful vision and ability to forecast the future and deal with it. He did so amidst an age of openness and prosperity whilst preserving the present and benefiting from the past in an exceptional approach and determination, Dr al Kaabi said, adding that he is a man of culture and courage.
"HH Sheikh Mohammed spares no efforts to lead a comprehensive progress and development in the UAE in light of the wise leadership of HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahayn, may Allah protect him, HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces, and Their Highnesses the Members of the Supreme Council, the Rulers of the Emirates" Dr al Kaabi added.
"He provides all that is necessary for enriching the cultural heritage in order to achieve excellence in all domains, empowering the UAE to compete for frontline ranks globally in innovation and excellence as well as in transfer of knowledge and culture to the whole world. He does this through an integrated comprehensive system and efforts aimed at promoting creativity and innovation, nurturing an ideal work environment and developing the country's infrastructure as per the international standards," Dr al Kaabi explained.   
"These achievements are the fruits of a vision that can strike a balance between the society's identity with its bright and creative heritage and the cross-cultural openness witnessed in our age," Dr al Kaabi underlined.
"The cultural domain in the UAE also has witnessed remarkable renaissance through HH Sheikh Mohammed's keen interest in this field, which reflects his innovative outlook that is aimed to render the UAE a leading country in drawing the cultural, artistic and economic landscape at the local, regional and international levels," Dr al Kaabi concluded.
