The Islamic Affairs has issued over 104 multilingual religious publications. They are released in Arabic, English and Urdu and came in implementation of Awqaf's strategic plan, approved by the UAE Cabinet, and in line with its quest to proficiently deliver its mission and vision to the community.
The publications included new editions of the holy Quran in various sizes, books to raise awareness of religious and Waqf (endowments) matters within the community, lessons of mosques and memorisation centres curriculums, as well as booklets on Hajj and Umrah, Ramadan, religious and national events celebrated in the country.
HE Dr Mohammed Matar Al Kaabi, Awqaf Chairman, highlighted that the entity was keen to achieve its vision and mission and promote the teachings of Islam. For this purpose, it makes the best use of various means of education and communication that are conducive to contributing to developing religious culture amongst people from all walks of life and conveying the bright image of the Islamic civilisation, he added.
Dr Al Kaabi praised the relentless support to Awqaf by HH Sheikh Khalifa Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the UAE President, HH Sheikh Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice President and Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai, HH Sheikh Mohammed Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces, and Their Highnesses Members of the Supreme Council and Rulers of the Emirates, highlighting their directives to promote the concepts of moderation and tolerance followed in the country.    
"Awqaf is keen to keep pace with developments relating to jurisprudential matters in all of its offerings to the public, be it through Friday sermons, lessons of the mosque, school curriculums, Quran memorisation centres, various seminars and publications provided by specialised scholars or the Official Fatwa Centre that also answers religious questions from the public. The Centre is staffed with qualified scholars who are abreast of developments in Fiqh matters," Dr Al Kaabi said.
