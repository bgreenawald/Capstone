The Islamic Affairs has confirmed that all arrangements and plans have been set for this year's season. Awqaf has laid down all plans that will contribute to achieving the Hajj mission's objectives and ensuring the maximum comfort for UAE's pilgrims. A preparatory committee leaves on September 3, while the official mission is set to leave on September 13, 2015. Both teams will return home on September 27. 
In a meeting with the heads of the mission's sub-committees held at Awqaf headoffice in Abu Dhabi, HE Dr Mohammed Matar Al Kaabi, Awqaf Chairman and Head of Hajj official mission, praised the support offered by HH Sheikh Khalifa Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, President of the UAE, HH Sheikh Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice President and Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai, HH Sheikh Mohammed Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces, and Their Highnesses Supreme Council Members and Rulers of the Emirates.
The meeting discussed the general tasks entrusted to heads of the committees as well as the processes for each committee and their plans, objectives, duties and tasks. Members of the committees were briefed on the new plans related to UAE pilgrims' camps at Mina and Arafat and improvements made thereof. According to the new plan, men and women were segregated, additional air conditioners and backup power generators were installed and the procedures of entry to the two camps were defined as well as various arrangements regarding Hajj campaigns at Muzdalifah camp.
