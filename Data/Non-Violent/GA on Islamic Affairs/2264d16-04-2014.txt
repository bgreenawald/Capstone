Islamic Affairs has promoted 313 of its staff to various grades in accordance to the Federal Authority for Government Human Resources' performance management system.
The prime goal is to lay out a metric approach to link rewards, incentives and bonuses to performance level and achievements. The system also aims at promoting a culture of positive performance that reflects the strategic directions of the federal government as per the resolutions it issued and subsequently approved by virtue of Cabinet decision no. 12 for 2012.
In a statement, Awqaf's chairman, HE Dr Hamdan Musallam al Mazrouei, said that the promotions came to motivate the personnel in return for their efforts and dedication, adding that it was a move to further encourage them to continue seeking excellence and increasing the efficiency of services provided by Awqaf to the community.
Dr al Mazrouei explained that all staff members are a centre of interest by the UAE wise leaders, who always direct to offer a working environment that is conducive to innovation and contributing to development and progress.  He also seized this occasion to praise the invaluable support to Awqaf from HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the UAE President, HH Sheikh Mohammad bin Rashid Al Maktoum, the UAE Vice President, Prime Minister and Ruler of Dubai, their Brothers, Their Highness The Rulers of the Emirates, and HH General Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, The Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces.
In its decision, Awqaf referred to the classifications and procedures based on three performance assessment criteria: significantly above expectations, above expectations and meeting expectations, respectively numbering 20, 254 and 39 employees.
