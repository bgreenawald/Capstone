Awqaf's Sheikha Salama Bint Batti Mosque in Al Ain won the first place at the level of the Gulf Cooperation Council countries in the second edition of Abdullatif Al Fozan Award for Mosque Architecture in the central mosques category. HH Prince Khalid Al Faisal, Emir of the Mecca Region, honoured Awqaf in presence of HH Sultan Bin Salman Al Saud and a host of senior officials from Islamic Affairs and mosque construction arenas in the GCC countries.
Commenting on the achievement, HE Dr Mohammed Matar al Kaabi, Awqaf's Chairman, said that Awqaf presented the award and the achievement to HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the UAE President, may Allah protect him, HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice President and Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai, HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces, and Their Highnesses the Ruler of the Emirates. Thanks to their great continuous support, mosques in the UAE have attained outstanding standing worldwide in terms of the smart and innovative Islamic architecture, Dr al Kaabi added, noting that such status was commended by the World Government Summit held recently in Dubai.
"This success further highlights the remarkable development in mosque construction in the UAE, thanks to the care given by its wise leadership, the aim being to glorify Islam and the houses of Allah, and to keep pace with the comprehensive urban development witnessed in the country," Dr al Kaabi concluded.
The award was received on behalf of HE Dr al Kaabi by Dr Abdul Aziz al Ghaithi, Director General of Awqaf's branch at al Ain.
