Awqaf honoured the winners of the 6th edition of its Holy Quran Memorisation and Intonation Contest the day before yesterday. The contest saw the participation of more than 1,000 contestants from the Awqaf-affiliated memorisation centres, inmates of punitive and correctional institutions and members of UAE special needs establishments in the 10 categories of the award. The winners were awarded cash prizes and certificates of excellence and appreciation.
The honouring ceremony, which was held in the Armed Forces Officers Club in Abu Dhabi, was attended by Dr Mohammed Matar al Kaabi, Chairman of Awqaf, guest scholars of the UAE President HH Sheikh Khalifa Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Awqaf executives, departmental directors and branch managers as well as guardians and students.
Dr Al Kaabi prayed to Allah Almighty to protect the President HH Sheikh Khalifa Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, HH Sheikh Mohammed Bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Vice President and Prime Minister of the UAE and Ruler of Dubai, and HH Sheikh Mohammed Bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces, to grant success to their Highnesses Supreme Council Members and Rulers of the Emirates, and have mercy upon the late Sheikh Zayed Bin Sultan Al Nahyan, whose graces are seen in every aspect of life, particularly with respect to paying due attention to religious affairs and taking care of the Holy Quran.
More than 1,000 male and female Holy Quran memorisers participated in this edition and 114 contestants qualified to the final stages, Dr al Kaabi said, noting that this edition saw the participation of the inmates of punitive and correctional institutions, the members of special needs establishments and all Holy Quran centers across the UAE.
Dr al Kaabi pointed out that Awqaf's Cabinet-approved strategic plan featured many initiatives, programmes and events aimed at organizing activities for teaching the Holy Quran, spreading awareness of the role of Holy Quran memorisation centres in the society and ensuring that the centres are fully equipped with the latest advanced means.
More than 1,000 male and female contestants joined the preliminary eliminations of the 6th edition of this contest, while 292 male and female contestants from all UAE Holy Quran memorisation centres and foundations were nominated for the initial eliminations.
