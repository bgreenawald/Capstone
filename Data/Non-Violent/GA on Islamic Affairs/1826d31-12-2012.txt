Awqaf contracted Al Mahala Contracting and General Transport Company (MCGTC) to build a new mosque in Bani Yas East, Abu Dhabi Emirate, at a cost of AED 5.5 mn.
HE Dr Mohammed Matar al Kaabi, Awqaf's director general, signed the contract on behalf of Awqaf, while on behalf of MCGTC Mr Ismail Fathi. The signing ceremony was attended by Mr Khaled Mohammed Sayf al Neyadi, the executive director of Waqf affairs.
Located in Bani Yas East 2/3, the new mosque can accommodate up to 800 worshippers. It will feature a modern design of architecture that complies to the specifications of Abu Dhabi Urban Planning Council. It is expected to be handed over within 13 months as of the starting date of construction work mentioned in the contract.
Among the mosque's facilities are a women's prayer hall with a capacity of 70 worshippers, accommodations for the Imam and the Muezzin, in addition to some annexes.
In a statement, Dr al Kaabi lauded the unlimited support to the construction and maintenance of mosques from HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the UAE President, may Allah protect him, his deputy HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, Prime Minister and Ruler of Dubai, their brothers Their Highness Rulers of the Emirates, and his trustworthy Crown Prince, HH General Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces. He also highlighted their continuous support to Awqaf and their keen interest in its various projects.
Dr al Kaabi added that Awqaf is determined to move forward in its strategic plan to build more mosques nationwide, in areas and residential districts that need such a facility. He also pointed out to the steady increase in the construction of mosques as part of Mafhas al Qatah programme, mentioning that donors are keen to contribute to various endowment projects undertaken by Awqaf.
