HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the Crown Prince of Abu Dhabi and Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces, stressed that serving the UAE pilgrims in the holy places is an utmost priority that receives special care and follow-up from the UAE wise leadership, headed by HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, UAE President, may Allah protect him.
HH Sheikh Khalifa's directives are always aimed at providing all facilities, support and capabilities to meet the UAE pilgrims' requirements and help them perform their Hajj rites in an easy manner and ensure their safety and security, HH Sheikh Mohammed added.
HH Sheikh Mohammed's remarks came while receiving the head and members of the UAE Official Hajj Mission at Al Bahr Palace.
HH praised the efforts of the mission in adopting effective standards to ensure prompt performance and excellence in delivering the best services and means to pilgrims, pointing out that serving pilgrims is a responsibility, honour and duty that require all to double their efforts, commitment and dedication in delivering the best services to pilgrims.
Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed also urged the mission to continue its efforts aimed at providing better services, initiatives and programmes to pilgrims, from their registration to their safe return home, and to work as a team to enable pilgrims to perform their rituals with tranquillity and safely, that they may, by Allah's grace, win His rewards.
Services and facilities
For his part, Dr Mohammed Matar al Kaabi, Awqaf's Chairman and Head of the official Hajj mission, briefed HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed on the services and facilities provided by the mission to UAE pilgrims by the mission to pilgrims during this Hajj season.
Dr Al Kaabi also informed HH about the plans and programmes the Awqaf intends to implement in the future.
Al Kaabi further hailed the interest of the UAE under the wise leadership of HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, UAE President, may Allah protect him, in providing the best services to pilgrims and commended Sheikh Mohamed's follow-up on the pilgrims' well-being throughout the Hajj season as well as his directives for harnessing all capabilities and facilities to serve them.
