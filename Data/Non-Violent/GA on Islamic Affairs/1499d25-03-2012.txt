Upon the demise of Pope Shenouda III of Alexandria and Patriarch of Saint Mark Episcopate, the Cathedral of Saint Antony for Coptic Orthodox in Abu Dhabi held a condolence gathering in which HE Dr Hamdan Musallam al Mazrouei, the GAIAE' Chairman, and the Awqaf delegation accompanying him extended their condolences to Father Issac Al Anba Bishoy, the Pastor at the Cathedral in Abu Dhabi.
The ceremony was also attended by Mr al Sayed Ali al Hashemi, the advisor on judicial and religious advisors at the Ministry of Presidential Affairs and Mr Tamer Mansour, the Egyptian Ambassador to the UAE, a delegation from the Egyptian Embassy, a number of Muslim scholars, heads of Christian  communities in Abu Dhabi, a number of  Egyptian residents, Muslims and Christians  and the Egyptian Women's Association, who came to present their condolences.
Dr al Mazrouei  praised the attributes of late Pope Shenouda III and his efforts throughout his life to work for upholding the true faith and adhering to a policy based on religious tolerance coexistence among all faiths.
Mr al Hashemi highlighted the depth of the relationship and convergence between the teachings and values of Islam and Christianity,  stressing the fact that the coexistence among followers of different faiths in UAE was the result of a sincere  and wise pursuit by the late sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, may Allah rest his soul in peace.
The same approach has been followed by HH Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the President of the UAE, may Allah protect him, HH Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum, the UAE Vice President, Prime Minister and Ruler of Dubai, Their Highness  Members of the Supreme Council, The Rulers of the Emirates, and HH General Sheikh Mohammed bin Zayed Al Nahyan, the Deputy Supreme Commander of the UAE Armed Forces.
For his part, Mr Mansour extended his thanks to the UAE leadership and people for hosting the Egyptian community, both Muslims and Copts, and for presenting their solemn condolences upon the decease of Pope Shenouda III, who represents a national symbol for all Egyptians and Arabs.
